Experience

Experience in Outworld comes from combat and active in-character play. Weak content falls off. Staying close to your group matters. Progress comes from moving forward, not farming the same safe area forever.

Core Summary

There are two ways to gain XP: combat XP and RP XP. Combat XP rewards kills that still match your stage of progression. RP XP is a timed reward for being in active IC areas with other players.

Combat XP

  • Killing relevant enemies gives XP.
  • Outlevel old content and its XP drops off.
  • To keep progressing, move into harder regions.

Shared XP

  • Party members can gain shared XP from kills.
  • You must be nearby, in the same area, and in the same faction.
  • Large level gaps reduce the value of shared XP.

RP XP

  • RP XP is awarded on a timer, not per action.
  • You must be in an IC area.
  • You must have other players in the area with you.
Important: if content is too easy for you, expect little or no meaningful XP from it.

Combat XP

Combat XP is straightforward. Kill enemies that are still appropriate for your progression, and you gain XP. Stay in content you have already outgrown, and the returns drop.

This means progression is region-based in practice. If you want efficient XP, move into content that still fights back.

Rule of thumb: if an area feels trivial, it is probably no longer good XP.

Shared XP

Shared XP exists so groups can progress together, but it has clear limits. You must be in the same faction, in the same area, and within 20 meters of the kill.

Shared XP is also weaker than getting the kill yourself, and it gets worse when the level gap is too large. The penalty begins once the party member is 2 or more levels below the killer.

Good shared XP

You are close to the fight, in the same area, fighting with people near your own level.

Bad shared XP

You are trailing behind, far from the kill, or relying on much higher-level players to carry you.

RP XP

RP XP ticks every 5 minutes. To receive it, you must be in an IC area with at least one other player present.

More players in the area increase the payout, up to a cap of 5 players for the multiplier. Being alone does not qualify. Sitting in an OOC area does not qualify.

Practical takeaway: RP XP rewards actual shared world presence, not solo idling.

How To Level Efficiently

  • Move on when a region stops challenging you.
  • Stay within 20 meters of your group if you want shared XP.
  • Do not expect carries to replace real progression.
  • Spend time in IC areas with other players for RP XP.
  • Expect steady progression, not huge bursts.

Quick Answers

  • Do weak enemies keep giving good XP forever? No.
  • How close do I need to be for shared XP? Within 20 meters.
  • Do I need to be in the same area? Yes.
  • Do I need to be in the same faction? Yes.
  • When does the shared XP level-gap penalty start? At 2 levels below the killer.
  • How often does RP XP tick? Every 5 minutes.
  • Does RP XP work in OOC areas? No.
  • Does being around more players help RP XP? Yes, up to 5 players.
  • Can I get RP XP while alone? No.
