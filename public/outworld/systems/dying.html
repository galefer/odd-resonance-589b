Dying

In Outworld, dropping to the ground is not the same as being gone forever. Most of the time, when you reach 0 HP or lower, you enter a critical state known as bleedout. From there, you still have a chance to recover, be saved, or stabilize before death finally claims you.

Core Summary

When you fall below 0 HP, you begin bleeding out. From that moment on, you repeatedly roll death saves until one of two things happens: you stabilize and recover, or your condition collapses and you die at -9.

Bleedout

  • Reaching 0 HP or lower usually puts you into bleedout instead of killing you instantly.
  • You make a death save every 20 seconds.
  • Each failed save moves you deeper by -1.

Stabilization

  • Passing a death save stabilizes you.
  • Once stabilized, your body slowly recovers.
  • When your HP rises above 0, you stand back up at 1 HP.

Death

  • If bleedout reaches -9, you die.
  • Whether that death is permanent depends on Memory.
  • If you have enough Memory, Rebirth may still be possible.
Important: you can enter bleedout multiple times. There is currently no penalty simply for going down repeatedly. The real question is whether you can recover each time.

How Does Dying Work

Bleedout gives you a window to survive, but it is still extremely dangerous. While you are down, you have no control over the situation and every failed save pushes you closer to death.

Enemies will often lose interest once you fall, but not always. If you are struck while down, things escalate quickly. A hit while bleeding out effectively pushes you further toward death, and if you had stabilized, it immediately breaks that stability.

This is why support and teamwork matter. A player on the ground can still be saved, but only if someone is ready to help.

In plain terms

  • You roll a save every 20 seconds.
  • Failing pushes you down by -1.
  • Passing stabilizes you.
  • At -9, you die.
  • Getting hit while down is extremely dangerous.
  • Healing above 0 ends bleedout immediately.

Death Saves & Survival Resources

Death saves are not fixed. Your current survival resources directly affect how hard they are.

The base death save DC is 20. From there, Vitality, Integrity, and Sanity each push that number up or down depending on how healthy they are.

50 is the neutral point. For every 10 points above 50, that resource makes the save 1 easier. For every 10 points below 50, it makes the save 1 harder. All three resources are counted together.

What this means in practice: if your condition is healthy, you are much more likely to stabilize. If your resources are run down, bleedout becomes far more dangerous.

You do not need to obsess over the exact math while playing. The simple rule is: healthy resources make recovery easier, neglected resources make death more likely.

This is why entering a fight already exhausted, exposed, or mentally frayed is so risky. You are not just weaker while standing, you are also less likely to get back up once dropped.

Simple rule

  • 20 is the base DC.
  • 50 in a resource is neutral.
  • Every +10 above 50 makes the save easier.
  • Every -10 below 50 makes the save harder.
  • All three survival resources matter.

Recovery

Bleedout can end in more ways than your own death saves. Any healing that brings you above 0 HP will immediately pull you out of bleedout.

Healing kits, spells, and prepared party members can bring you back before your condition spirals toward death. This is why well-stocked groups survive far longer than unprepared ones.

In practice, success is not only about dealing damage. It is about making sure someone can pull you back up when things go wrong.

Practical takeaway: when adventuring in dangerous areas, assume someone will eventually hit the floor and plan accordingly.

Your Condition Matters

Your odds of surviving bleedout depend heavily on the state you were in before you fell. The healthier your survival resources are, the easier it is to recover.

Entering a fight already exhausted, injured, or depleted makes dying far more dangerous. See Survival Resources for more on those systems.

Where Memory Fits

This page explains what happens when you fall. What happens after death is governed by Memory.

If bleedout collapses into death, Memory determines whether you still have another chance through Rebirth.

In simple terms: bleedout decides whether you survive the moment. Memory decides how many times you can afford not to.

Quick Answers

  • Do I die immediately at 0 HP? Usually no. You enter bleedout.
  • How often do death saves happen? Every 20 seconds.
  • What is the base death save DC? 20.
  • How do survival resources affect it? Healthy resources lower the DC. Poor resources raise it.
  • What happens when I fail? You drop by -1.
  • What happens when I pass? You stabilize and begin recovering.
  • What ends bleedout instantly? Any healing that brings you above 0 HP.
  • When do I actually die? At -9.
  • What determines if I can come back? See Memory and Rebirth.
