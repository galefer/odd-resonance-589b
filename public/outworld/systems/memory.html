Memory

You have Memory. In Outworld, Memory is the currency of your existence. It is what lets you respawn through Rebirth, and it is also what ties you to the life you had before arrival. Spend too much of it, and you do not just lose a resource. You lose yourself.

Core Summary

Memory is something you should protect. You start with 80%, already diminished by arrival. Rebirth costs 25 Memory. If you ever die without enough left to return, that death is permanent.

What it is

  • Your remaining connection to the person you were before arriving.
  • A persistent resource that matters beyond a single death.
  • Both a gameplay currency and a roleplay truth.

What spends it

  • Rebirth costs 25 Memory.
  • Parting with Memory is never trivial. It is literally giving up pieces of your old self.
  • Each return carries a real personal cost.

What is at stake

  • Not enough Memory to Rebirth means permanent death.
  • You should not assume you can always recover what was lost.
  • Memory recovery exists, but it is rare enough that you should never plan around it.
Important: Memory is not just another currency to spend efficiently. It is one of the most personal resources in Outworld. When you part with it, you are parting with your past.

What Players Should Actually Care About

Memory is your long-term survival margin. Death & Bleedout decides whether you go down. Rebirth decides whether you come back. Memory is what pays that price.

That means Memory changes the tone of every risk you take. A rough expedition is not just about whether you survive the fight. It is also about whether the loss is worth another piece of your identity.

The safest mindset is simple: hold onto Memory whenever you can. You will be tempted to spend it, and sometimes you will have to, but you should treat it as precious, not disposable.

In plain terms

  • You begin at 80%.
  • Rebirth costs 25 Memory.
  • Memory does not go below 0.
  • No Memory means no more coming back.
  • Rare ways to recover Memory exist, but you should not bank on them.
  • The lower it gets, the less of your old self remains.

Memory & Identity

Memory is not only mechanical. It changes who your character is able to be. The following ranges are a roleplay guide for how much of your pre-arrival life still feels real to you.

Memory Roleplay feel
81–100% You have great recall. You mostly remember everything from before arrival. Small background details may blur now and then, but nothing important feels lost.
61–80% You have good recall. Most of your past is still there, but cracks are beginning to show. A name may slip. A face may come to mind less clearly than it should.
41–60% Your old life feels foggier. You can still remember it, but less-critical details often take effort to retrieve. You catch yourself straining to hold onto things that once came naturally.
21–40% Pre-arrival has become distant. The broad themes of your life remain, but the specifics are blurred. It feels like an adult trying to recall early childhood: real, but hazy and incomplete.
1–20% Very little remains. You remember your name, and sometimes you catch brief flashes of your past, but they are fleeting. Most of who you were is gone.
0% Your life before the Dead World is gone. You may know your old name if others tell you, but it feels foreign. Many at this point reject it entirely and take a nickname, a title, or no name at all.

Roleplay Guide

High Memory

At the high end, your past still feels like yours. You can name people, places, loyalties, habits, and beliefs without feeling like you are reaching into fog.

Mid Memory

In the middle ranges, the shape of your old life survives, but the details start slipping. You know what mattered. You may no longer remember why with perfect clarity.

Low Memory

At the low end, identity becomes fractured. Your past arrives as fragments instead of continuity. You are no longer carrying a life. You are carrying scraps.

Empty Memory

At 0%, pre-arrival is effectively alien. Even if someone tells you who you were, it no longer feels like truth you can inhabit. That person is gone.

RP note: the exact details are yours to play with, but the direction is clear. Lower Memory means weaker connection to your old life, weaker ownership of your old name, and weaker continuity of self.

How To Treat Memory

Do not think of Memory as a potion stack you can refill later. Think of it as a reserve of selfhood. Spending it may be necessary, but it should never feel free.

Outworld is built to tempt you into parting with it. That tension is intentional. The system wants Rebirth to feel available, but never cheap.

The best way to survive it is to respect the cost, avoid reckless deaths, travel prepared, and remember that every unnecessary return makes the next one more dangerous.

Quick Answers

  • How much Memory do I start with? 80%.
  • What is Memory used for? Most importantly, Rebirth.
  • How much does Rebirth cost? 25 Memory.
  • Can Memory go below 0? No.
  • Can I rely on getting Memory back? No. Recovery exists, but it is rare and should never be your plan.
  • What happens if I die without enough Memory to Rebirth? Permanent death.
  • Where do I read about going down in combat? See Death & Bleedout.
