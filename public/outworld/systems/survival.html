Survival Resources

The pillars of survival are Vitality, Integrity, and Sanity. These are long-term condition bars that reflect how well your character is holding together. What matters is what they mean, how they affect you, and what happens when you let them slip.

Core Summary

Survival resources sit on a 0 to 100 scale and are always active while you are playing normally. High values keep you strong. Low values start dragging your character down. Left unchecked, they can turn a stable situation into a collapse.

Vitality

  • Your basic physical well-being.
  • Represents things like hunger, thirst, fatigue, and bodily endurance.
  • If Vitality fully bottoms out, you start taking direct damage over time.

Integrity

  • Your body's resistance to exposure, corruption, and physical destabilisation.
  • This is the resource most likely to be pressured while spending time outside while the sun is up.
  • Being indoors, or outside during the night, is safe for Integrity.

Sanity

  • Your mental stability.
  • Sanity reflects how well you are coping with stress, fear, and the state of your body.
  • If your physical condition starts failing, Sanity is usually not far behind.
Important: these resources are not just flavor bars. They directly affect your stats, your resilience, and your chances of getting back up after being knocked down.

What Players Should Actually Care About

The system is meant to be felt through pressure. If you spend too long exposed to daylight, travel while already run down, or keep pushing without recovery, your bars start slipping.

Once they slip far enough, your character stops feeling reliable. Saves get worse. Core stats begin to suffer. You become easier to put down, and harder to recover.

The key thing to understand is simple: healthy resources support each other, neglected resources drag each other down. You are safest when all three are being maintained together, not when one is being ignored because the others still look fine.

In plain terms

  • High resources mean a stronger, steadier character.
  • Low resources mean worse saves and weaker stats.
  • Integrity is pressured by daylight exposure, not by being indoors or travelling outside at night.
  • Vitality hitting 0 starts causing real HP loss.
  • Bad physical condition also hurts your mental condition.
  • The system becomes much more dangerous once multiple bars are low at once.

Thresholds & Stat Effects

Each resource has four bands. You do not need to know the internals, but you should know the feel of them. Above 75 is strong. Above 50 is stable. Below 50 is the point where weakness starts showing. Below 25 is severe.

Resource 75+ 50–74 25–49 0–24
Vitality +2 Constitution, +2 Strength +1 Fortitude save -1 Fortitude save -2 Constitution, -2 Strength
Integrity +2 Dexterity, +2 Intelligence +1 Reflex save -1 Reflex save -2 Dexterity, -2 Intelligence
Sanity +2 Wisdom, +2 Charisma +1 Will save -1 Will save -2 Wisdom, -2 Charisma
Real warning line: treat 50 as the danger point, not 25. Once a bar drops below 50, you are no longer in a comfortable range. Once it drops below 25, the system stops being forgiving.

Death Save Relation

Survival resources are also tied to your death saves. The healthier your resources are, the easier it is for your character to fight their way back up after being knocked down. The worse your condition is, the less likely you are to recover cleanly.

This page is not the full death save breakdown, but you should treat resource management as part of staying alive in combat, not just staying alive between combats. For the dedicated breakdown, see Dying.

Restoring Resources

Food is one of the main ways to raise or lower these resources. Different foods affect Vitality, Integrity, and Sanity in different ways.

That means sustenance is not just about eating anything available. What you choose to consume matters. Some foods are better for physical recovery, some support mental stability, and some help keep your body together against exposure. Others may come with tradeoffs.

If you want the full breakdown of how food and consumables interact with these systems, read Sustenance.

Simple rule: if your resources are slipping, look at what you are eating. Recovery is part planning, not just survival.

How To Survive The System

This system is punishing on purpose, but it is not meant to be unfair. Its job is to pace you, pressure your decisions, and force you to stay mindful of your condition.

The best way to survive is to stay above the curve. Do not wait until all three bars are crashing before you react. Once you fall into a major deficit, the system starts compounding against you, and dragging yourself back out can be slow, expensive, or require help from other players.

Take breaks from combat. Make sure you are grinding or carrying the right food and cure items. Respect your limits instead of constantly pushing through them. Staying stable is fair, intended, and much easier than trying to recover from a full spiral.

Survival mindset: this is not a system you brute force forever. Good players manage pressure early, keep supplies on hand, and avoid turning a manageable problem into a collapse.

The HUD

The survival HUD shows your three resources as bars: Vitality, Integrity, and Sanity. The fuller and greener a bar is, the healthier that resource is. As it drops, the bar shifts toward red.

The HUD appears during normal in-character play and disappears in places or states where the system is not relevant. It exists to give you a quick read on your condition, not bury you in numbers.

Vitality tooltip

Physical well-being: hunger, thirst, fatigue, and survival needs.

Integrity tooltip

Physical stability against warp exposure and bodily degradation.

Sanity tooltip

Mental stability against fear, stress, and anomalous influence.

What This Feels Like In Play

Well maintained

When your resources are healthy, your character feels solid. Your stats are supported, your saves are cleaner, and being knocked down is less likely to turn into a disaster.

Worn down

When one bar starts slipping, you may still function, but you are no longer at your best. Once multiple bars are low together, the system starts compounding against you.

Exposure pressure

Daylight exposure is not free. When the sun is up, spending time outside puts pressure on Integrity first and can start pulling the rest of your condition down with it. Being indoors, or outside during the night, is safe for Integrity.

Vitality collapse

If Vitality reaches 0, the danger becomes immediate. You begin taking 1 damage over time until the situation is corrected.

Best habit: do not wait until a bar is nearly empty. By the time things look disastrous, the penalties are already biting.

Quick Answers

  • Can these resources go over 100? No. They are capped between 0 and 100.
  • Can they drop below 0? No. They floor at 0.
  • Do I instantly die at 0? No. Hitting 0 is dangerous, but it is not instant death by itself under the current rules.
  • What happens at 0 Vitality? You begin taking 1 damage over time.
  • How do I recover these resources? Mainly through food and consumables. Different foods can raise or lower different resources. See Sustenance.
  • What should I watch most closely? Any bar dropping below 50, especially if more than one is slipping at once.
